## What Sprout Social Actually Does

Sprout Social is one of the best tools in its category — social media management for teams. Core capabilities:

- **Publishing:** Schedule and publish posts across Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, X, TikTok, and more from a single dashboard
- **Content calendar:** Plan your posting schedule, collaborate on drafts, manage approval workflows
- **Analytics:** Track engagement, follower growth, post performance, and competitive benchmarking
- **Listening:** Monitor brand mentions, hashtags, and keywords across social platforms
- **Inbox management:** Unified inbox for responding to comments and DMs across platforms
- **Team features:** Multi-user, role-based access, approval workflows, team performance reporting

Sprout Social is excellent if your job is to run a social media operation — managing a content calendar, keeping a posting cadence, tracking how your content performs, and coordinating a team around it.

**Where it doesn't go:** Sprout's social listening shows you when your brand is mentioned. It doesn't find unbranded conversations — the thousands of comment threads happening right now on posts that have nothing to do with your brand, where someone is asking "does anyone know a good [product in your category]?"

## What Signal Harvester Actually Does

Signal Harvester is built for one specific problem: finding people on Instagram who are already in a buying moment, before your competitors find them.

The mechanism: Instagram's comment sections are filled with intent signals. Someone posts a fitness photo and 40 commenters ask what protein powder they use. A travel blogger posts a trip and hundreds of comments ask about travel insurance. A food creator posts a recipe and comments ask about the air fryer they used.

Most of these intent signals are invisible to the brands that could respond to them — they're scattered across thousands of posts, buried in notification feeds, and unreachable at scale without a tool.

Signal Harvester surfaces them. You define the intent patterns for your product ("asking for recommendations," "expressing a problem," "looking for alternatives") and the platform scans comment activity to find matching signals in real time. You respond first. You win the sale.

**Where it doesn't go:** Signal Harvester is not a content scheduling tool. It doesn't manage your posting calendar, track your follower analytics, or coordinate your team's social workflows. It does one thing: find the people who are already looking to buy what you sell.
